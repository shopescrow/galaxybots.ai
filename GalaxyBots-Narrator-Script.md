# GalaxyBots New User Guide — Narrator Script
### Synthesia.io — 13 Slides

---

## Slide 1 — Title: Your AI Executive Team Starts Here

Welcome to GalaxyBots dot A-I — Fortune 500 intelligence, at your command.

In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to get started with your personal AI executive team. By the end of these steps, you'll know how to log in, connect with your bots, deploy a full team for complex projects, and stay in control through the Command Center. Let's get started.

---

## Slide 2 — Step 1: Log In

Step one is logging in. Start by navigating to your GalaxyBots URL — this is the link provided by your administrator when your account was set up.

Once you're there, enter your email address and the password you created during onboarding, then click Sign In.

If your organization uses Single Sign-On, there's nothing extra to do. GalaxyBots automatically detects your email domain and routes you through your company's SSO provider — no separate login is required.

---

## Slide 3 — Step 2: Your Command Center

Once you're signed in, you'll land in the Command Center — this is your operations hub.

Everything happening across your AI team is visible here. The Activity Feed gives you a live log of every action your bots are taking. Pending Approvals shows you any actions that require your sign-off before they proceed. And Company Status gives you a real-time view of your entire fleet's health at a glance.

Think of the Command Center as mission control — this is where you stay informed and in charge.

---

## Slide 4 — Step 3: Meet Your Bot Team

Step three is meeting your team. GalaxyBots comes pre-loaded with a full corporate roster of AI executives, each specialized in a distinct business function.

Your CFO Bot handles financial analysis, P&L review, cost optimization, and budget forecasting — your finance executive, on demand. The Marketing Director covers campaign strategy, brand positioning, content planning, and competitive intelligence. And the CMO Bot drives go-to-market strategy, lead generation, Prospector pipeline management, and growth analysis.

These are just three of your specialists — explore the full Bots section to see your entire roster.

---

## Slide 5 — Step 4: Open a Channel

Step four is opening a direct channel with a specialist. In the left sidebar, navigate to Bots. Browse your roster and select the specialist you want to work with — in this example, the CMO Bot from the Growth Department.

Once you've selected your bot, click "Open Channel." That's it. Your secure, private line to that executive is now live and ready for instructions.

Each channel is dedicated to that bot, keeping your conversations focused and your history organized.

---

## Slide 6 — Step 5: Send Your Directive

Step five is sending your first directive. Your bots understand plain language — you don't need to use special commands or formats. Just tell them what you need, and they'll interpret and act.

For example, you might say: "Analyze our Q2 revenue and flag areas of concern." Your CFO Bot responds immediately: it pulls the Q2 data, identifies that margin compression in Operations is down four point two percent versus Q1, and flags three specific cost centers for your review.

Be specific, be direct, and your bots will deliver.

---

## Slide 7 — Pro Tip: Enable Deep Thinking Mode

Here's an important pro tip. For complex, strategic analysis, enable Deep Thinking Mode before you send your directive.

When Deep Thinking is active, your bot synthesizes ten independent AI perspectives in parallel — examining your question from multiple angles simultaneously before delivering a consolidated answer. This is designed for decisions that demand depth: market entry analysis, risk assessment, competitive strategy, or any situation where a single perspective isn't enough.

Enable it whenever the stakes are high and the answer needs to be right.

---

## Slide 8 — Step 6: Deploy a Team

Step six is deploying a full team for larger, multi-department objectives. Rather than working with one bot at a time, Deploy Team assembles a coordinated group of specialists around a single goal.

The process has four stages. First, you define your objective — describe what needs to be accomplished. Then Optima Prime, your AI Commander, analyzes that objective and assembles the right team. The correct specialists are selected automatically. Finally, a Task Room is launched where all your bots work together in one shared space toward one goal.

---

## Slide 9 — Optima Prime: Your AI Commander

Optima Prime is the intelligence layer behind every team deployment. When you submit an objective, Optima Prime doesn't just pick a list of bots — it analyzes your goal across all departments to understand which expertise is actually required.

It then proposes the right specialists for that specific task, based on what the work demands. And if your objective calls for a capability that isn't covered by your current roster, Optima Prime will suggest new bots to fill those gaps.

Optima Prime ensures every team you deploy is precisely matched to the mission.

---

## Slide 10 — Step 7: Lead Your Task Boardroom

Step seven is leading your Task Boardroom. Once your team is deployed, you enter a shared workspace where you can direct the full team simultaneously in one place.

You're in charge. You can assign tasks, set priorities, and redirect focus at any time. If mid-session you realize you need an additional specialist, you can request them to be added without interrupting the flow. And every action your bots take is tracked in real time — nothing happens without a record.

The Boardroom is where strategy becomes execution.

---

## Slide 11 — Step 8: Managing Approvals

Step eight is managing approvals. Your bots are powerful, and for consequential actions — like sending communications, making recommendations, or executing tasks on your behalf — they'll request your approval before proceeding.

The flow is straightforward: your bots surface a request for action. You review it in the Command Center under Pending Approvals. Then you either approve, and the bot proceeds, or reject, and the action is stopped.

This keeps you in full control of what your AI team does on your behalf, with a complete audit trail of every decision.

---

## Slide 12 — Closing: You're Ready to Lead

You're ready to lead. Your AI executive team is standing by.

You now know how to connect with individual bots, deploy full teams through Optima Prime, collaborate in the Task Boardroom, and govern every action through the Command Center. Your three core tools — Bots for direct conversations, Deploy Team for coordinated missions, and Command Center for oversight — are all within reach.

GalaxyBots dot A-I: Fortune 500 intelligence, at your command. Welcome to your team.

## Slide 13 — Quick Start Reference Card

Use this Quick Start card as your go-to reference whenever you need a fast reminder.

There are eight steps: Log in using your GalaxyBots URL and credentials. Check the Command Center for your activity feed, approvals, and company status. Visit Bots in the left nav to explore your full roster. Select a specialist and click Open Channel for a direct line. Send your directive in plain language — your bots act immediately. Use Deploy Team for complex goals that need multiple specialists. Lead your team in the Task Boardroom for collaborative missions. And review all bot actions in Pending Approvals before they execute.

Your key locations: Command Center, Bots, Deploy Team, Boardroom, and Approvals. Everything you need, right at your fingertips.
