To launch "The Liberator" as a flagship product for GalaxyBots.ai, you need to pivot from "automation tool" to "strategic exit asset." The core message is that you are not just migrating data—you are breaking vendor lock-in.

Below is a comprehensive marketing plan structured to generate leads, build trust, and close sales for B2B SaaS buyers.

1. Product & Positioning Strategy (The "Why")
Your unique selling proposition is Switching Cost Arbitrage. While competitors focus on API integrations, you focus on "Visual Liberation."

The Hook: "Stop paying ransom to your CRM. Get your data out in one click, without developers."

The Target Persona: Founders, RevOps leaders, and IT managers stuck in legacy contracts (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive) who want to migrate to cheaper or better alternatives but fear data loss.

The Niche Angle: Position this as "The Moving Truck" for SaaS. You don't need the new landlord's permission (API) to move your furniture out.

2. The "Zero Trust" Content Engine (Demand Generation)
Since the tool works via screen recording and AI vision, trust is your biggest hurdle. You must prove you don't steal data.

Video Asset: "The 5-Minute Heist": A screen recording showing the agent scraping a dummy database live. Show the raw CSV being generated in real-time.

Interactive Calculator: "The Switching Cost Calculator." Input: Current CRM price + custom report costs + IT hours. Output: Money saved by moving to a competitor.

Whitepaper: "The Great Export: Why APIs are a Trap and Vision is the Future." This establishes thought leadership on data sovereignty.

3. Launch Sequence (The "Galactic" Onboarding)
Use a 14-day challenge format to prove efficacy.

Day 1-3: "The Scout" (Free Audit). User provides read-only access or shares a screen recording (via your tool). GalaxyBots.ai sends back a free "Data Topography Map" (a visual of all their hidden tables and attachments) within 24 hours. Value: Shows you understand their mess.

Day 4-7: "The Blockade Run" (Sandbox). User runs the Liberator on a small subset (e.g., 50 contacts). You export to a Google Sheet.

Day 8-14: "The Exodus" (Conversion). User pays for full extraction.

4. Channel & Distribution Strategy
LinkedIn "Ghost in the Machine" Campaign:

Post Idea: "Your CRM doesn't own your data. It's just holding it hostage. Here is a Python script using GPT-4V to visually extract every single row without asking permission." (Give away a limited script to go viral).

Partnerships with "Alternatives To" Sites:

Advertise on pages comparing "HubSpot vs. Salesforce." Your ad copy: "Stuck? Liberate your data before you switch."

Agency White-Labeling:

Market to marketing agencies who are sick of rebuilding dashboards every time a client fires their old vendor.

5. Pricing Model (The "Ransom" vs. "Liberation")
Tier	Price	Target
Scout	$0	DIY Freelancers (Export to CSV only)
Liberator	$499 / migration	SMBs (Preserves relationships & attachments)

6. Launch Week Tactics (The "Heist" Launch)
Do not do a boring launch. Do a "Heist" launch.

The Landing Page: Dark mode, green "Matrix" code rain, terminal-style text. Headline: > sudo ./liberate --crm=salesforce --target=airtable

Live Demo on Product Hunt: Record a live session where you type a command and watch the bot literally "click" through a real UI (a dummy CRM like HubSpot's test account) faster than a human.

7. Objection Handling Script (For Sales Calls)
Q: Is this legal?

A: "Under GDPR and CCPA, you have the Right to Data Portability. We are simply exercising that right at a machine level, not a human level. We don't hack; we automate your clicks."

Q: What if the UI changes mid-migration?

A: "The AI doesn't look for code (XPath); it looks for visual patterns (the 'Next' button shape). If it gets lost, it pauses and alerts you. You own the outcome, not the script."

8. Success Metrics (KPIs)
Completion Rate: >95% of migrations finish without manual intervention.

Time-to-Value: Average time from signup to "Data Downloaded" < 4 hours.

The "Freedom" Score: NPS survey asking "How much did we reduce your switching anxiety?" (Scale 1-10).
